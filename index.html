Projects

Production Line Intelligence & Rework Risk Modeling

91% Rework Risk Recall · 90% Rework-Cause Accuracy · 9% Baseline Rework Rate

This project simulates a multi-stage manufacturing operation to analyze production performance, downtime, cycle time, and rework risk. The dashboard highlights where production delays and quality issues appear across machines, shifts, and product families.

The goal was to move beyond static reporting and create an operational decision-support view: identifying high-risk units before failure, surfacing rework drivers, and giving leaders earlier visibility into problems that could affect throughput, quality, and cost.

Global Supply Chain & Procurement Intelligence

$174.9M Total Spend · 85.7% Spend Concentrated in Top 5 Suppliers · 95.2% OTIF · 2.7% Defect Rate

This project analyzes global procurement performance to help leaders understand spend concentration, supplier dependency, delivery reliability, and quality risk. The dashboard shows that five suppliers account for 85.7% of total spend, with the largest supplier representing $63.6M, or 36.3%, of purchasing activity.

The goal was to turn procurement data into a decision-support view for sourcing strategy, supplier conversations, and risk management. By connecting spend, OTIF, defect rate, and cost of poor quality, the dashboard helps identify where procurement decisions may affect cost, quality, and supply chain reliability.

Mapping Water Bodies for Drought Relief

Satellite Data · NDWI Trends · Drought Hotspots · Public-Sector Analytics

This project turns satellite and geospatial data into a drought-relief planning tool for La Guajira, Colombia. Using NDWI, clustering, water-proximity analysis, and interactive maps, the project identifies drought-prone zones and evaluates how accessible nearby water sources may be.

The analysis showed persistent dryness across the 2020–2024 NDWI trend, with average monthly NDWI remaining below the 0.2 water-presence threshold. DBSCAN revealed one dominant drought-related cluster with 52,689 points, while smaller clusters pointed to localized drought pockets that may need targeted intervention.

The project connects environmental analytics with public-sector decision support: helping translate raw satellite data, water-body detection, and drought patterns into insights that could support resource allocation, emergency planning, and long-term water-management strategy.

About

My work sits at the intersection of operations, business intelligence, process improvement, and applied data science. I’m interested in the questions that actually affect decisions: Where are we losing time? What is driving rework? Which suppliers are creating risk? What patterns are hiding in the data? What should leaders act on next?

I use SQL, PostgreSQL, Tableau, Power BI, Python, and machine learning to turn those questions into dashboards, analysis, models, and recommendations people can use.

My background includes industrial engineering, an M.S. in Data Science from the University of Texas at Arlington, and MIT’s Applied Data Science Program. This portfolio brings those pieces together through projects focused on operations, BI, forecasting, process improvement, and decision support.

How I Work

I approach analytics from both a business intelligence and process-improvement perspective. I look for operational patterns in the data, translate them into measurable KPIs, and build dashboards or models that help leaders decide what to improve next.
